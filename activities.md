## BE-SAFE at the SPARK International Partnership Meeting — UBI, Covilhã
**April 22, 2026 · University of Beira Interior (UBI), Portugal**

The BE-SAFE project was presented at the **University of Beira Interior (UBI)** during the partners' meeting of the Interreg project **[SPARK](https://www.interregeurope.eu/spark)** (Smart Planning for Agile Urban Mobility Solutions). The presentation highlighted BE-SAFE's approach to enhancing cycling safety and promoting active mobility within smart city frameworks.

The event brought together international experts and decision-makers from **Portugal, Spain, Romania, Croatia, Moldova, Cyprus, Lithuania, and Bulgaria**, representing municipalities, intermunicipal communities, regional development agencies, and technology and innovation hubs. By participating in this forum, BE-SAFE reaffirms its commitment to placing cycling safety at the heart of European smart city mobility policies.

## 1st In-Person Meeting — Brașov, Romania
**March 13–14, 2026 · Universitatea Transilvania din Brașov (UniTBV)**

The BE-SAFE consortium held its first in-person midpoint meeting at UniTBV in Brașov, Romania. The two-day event reviewed the results of the first six months of the project and set the direction for the next phase.

### Day 1 · March 13

**Session I — Results & Ongoing Execution (Tasks 1, 2 & 3)**
- Welcome from the local host, UniTBV
- Overall project status update (UBI)
- **Task 1 (UBI):** presentation of accident risk maps and ongoing machine-learning–based predictive models for bicycle accident severity; update on Master's dissertation integration
- **Task 2 (UniTBV):** analysis of the psychological survey results, covering risk profiles and cyclist motivation
- **Task 3 (UPNA):** review of sensor installation protocols and status of real-time data collection (braking/acceleration patterns and V2X testing)

**Session II — Integration & Simulation (Tasks 4 & 5)**
- Data fusion strategy: integrating Task 1–3 outputs into the unified risk-assessment framework
- Simulation architecture: defining the SUMO–Veins–PLEXE toolchain for sensing, communication and road-infrastructure redesign
- Validation planning: parameters for small-scale pilot/laboratory testing (Task 5)

**Session III — Education & Dissemination**
- Abstract submitted to **ETC 2026** (European Transport Conference, Porto)
- Workshop integration proposal: *Introduction to GeoAI in GIS – Cyclist Safety* (GeoIA-EPIC, UBI) as a BE-SAFE educational activity
- Preliminary discussion on a **BIP Erasmus+ 2027–2028** joint Blended Intensive Programme
- Brainstorming session on the **Project Dissemination Microsite** (this website)
- Definition of authorship and core findings for the open-access journal article

**Session IV — Project Continuity, Funding Calls & New Partners**

| Programme | Focus | Window |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| UNITA Advancing Grants | Scaling BE-SAFE across the UNITA alliance | Mar 2 – Apr 17, 2026 |
| Erasmus+ Cooperation Partnerships | Smart City Safety Planners curriculum | Mar 2026 & Mar 2027 |
| EIT Urban Mobility | Municipal pilot testing of V2X / sensor strategy | Apr – Jun 2026 |
| Horizon Europe (Cluster 5) | Large-scale integration into EU Mission Cities | Sep – Oct 2026 |
| EU City Facility / URBACT | Physical infrastructure interventions from Task 4 | Early–Mid 2027 |

Potential new partners discussed:
- **University of Pau and Pays de l'Adour** (France) — Computer Science
- **HES-SO Haute École Spécialisée de Suisse Occidentale** (Switzerland) — Computer Science

### Day 2 · March 14

- Evaluation of the Call for **UNITA Infrastructure Grants**
- Pilot analysis of selected urban locations for field trials
- Cultural programme: visit to **Bran Castle**
